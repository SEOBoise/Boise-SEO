Things to do and places to visit in Boise, Idaho

Boise Art Museum

Whether you are an art enthusiast or simply looking to explore the unique culture of Boise, the Boise Art Museum is a must-visit destination. Situated in the heart of downtown, this hidden gem showcases an impressive collection of contemporary and modern art that is sure to captivate both locals and tourists alike. With its clean and minimalist design, the museum offers a welcoming and tranquil atmosphere, allowing visitors to fully immerse themselves in the world of art.

The Boise Art Museum prides itself on its commitment to promoting local artists, making it an essential platform for emerging talent in the region. From thought-provoking installations to visually stunning paintings, the museum curates a diverse range of artwork that reflects the dynamic artistic landscape of Boise. Offering a balanced blend of traditional and avant-garde pieces, the exhibitions here constantly push boundaries and challenge conventional notions of art. Whether you are a seasoned art connoisseur or simply seeking visual inspiration, the Boise Art Museum promises a memorable experience that will leave you with a newfound appreciation for the power of creativity.

Old Idaho Penitentiary

The dimly lit corridors of the Old Idaho Penitentiary echo with whispers of a dark and troubled past. Built in 1872, this formidable stone structure served as a place of confinement and punishment for some of Idaho's most notorious criminals. With its imposing walls and foreboding watchtowers, it stands as a testament to the harsh realities of crime and punishment in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Entering the cold and unforgiving cell blocks, one can almost feel the weight of the prisoners' despair that once permeated the air. The cramped cells, barely large enough to house a single inmate, provide a stark reminder of the harsh conditions endured by those who were incarcerated here. As visitors wander through the narrow hallways, they can't help but imagine the lives that were lost and the stories that remain untold within these walls.

World Center for Birds of Prey

Located in Boise, Idaho, the World Center for Birds of Prey is a remarkable institution dedicated to the conservation and study of raptors. It serves as a sanctuary for injured birds and conducts research to better understand these incredible creatures. The center's mission is not just limited to the conservation efforts; it aims to educate the public about the importance of raptor conservation and the role these birds play in maintaining the ecological balance of our planet. Through their various educational programs, visitors are given the opportunity to interact with these majestic creatures up close and personal.

One of the center's proudest accomplishments is its successful breeding program for endangered raptor species. With meticulous care and expertise, the staff at the World Center for Birds of Prey has been able to breed and release numerous individuals back into the wild, contributing to the restoration of their populations. The center takes immense pride in its role in promoting the survival of these magnificent birds, and their dedication is evident in the meticulous attention given to the details of each bird's care. Through the center's efforts, a glimmer of hope is brought to the conservation of these threatened species, ensuring a brighter future for them.

World Center for Birds of Prey



Bogus Basin Mountain Recreation Area

Nestled in the stunning beauty of the Boise National Forest, Bogus Basin Mountain Recreation Area is a true haven for outdoor enthusiasts. With its diverse terrain and abundant recreational opportunities, it offers an unforgettable experience for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. From hiking and mountain biking in the summer to skiing and snowboarding in the winter, this mountain playground has something for everyone.

One of the biggest draws of Bogus Basin is its extensive trail system. With over 70 miles of trails, hikers and bikers can explore the area to their heart's content. The trails range from easy strolls through meadows and forests to challenging uphill climbs that reward hikers with breathtaking views. No matter your skill level or preference, there is a trail for you at Bogus Basin. And with its proximity to Boise, it's the perfect day trip or weekend getaway for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and immerse themselves in nature's tranquility.
